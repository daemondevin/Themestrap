Getting started

The secondary navigation stays at the top of this frame as you scroll. Notice the shadow that appears once it becomes "stuck" — that is the .ts-navbar--stuck class, toggled by an IntersectionObserver watching a zero-height sentinel placed just before the bar.

Keep scrolling to confirm the bar remains pinned and the shadow persists. Scroll back to the top and the shadow disappears as the bar returns to its resting position flush with the content.

In a real MODX page you would set &sticky_offset to the height of your primary navigation so the secondary bar pins neatly below it instead of at the very top of the viewport.

The sticky behavior is opt-out: pass {"sticky": false} in the plugin options, or set &sticky=`0` on the snippet, to disable it entirely.

And that's the stuck state. The same mechanism works against the real viewport when the navbar is placed directly in a page rather than inside a scrolling frame.
