From two-day deploys to six minutes — how Velocity AI scaled to Series A with Launchpad

SR
Sasha Reeve
CTO, Velocity AI · Published March 2025
Velocity AI
San Francisco, CA · Series A
Customer since 2023 · Growth plan

In early 2023, Velocity AI was a three-person team building a machine learning inference platform. They had a product that worked. What they didn't have was the engineering infrastructure to ship it reliably — or fast enough to keep up with their roadmap.

The problem: two-day deployments in a market that moves hourly

Before Launchpad, deploying a new release at Velocity AI was an event. The team maintained a hand-rolled CI/CD pipeline built on a combination of GitHub Actions, a self-managed Kubernetes cluster, and an assortment of bash scripts that had accumulated over 18 months. On a good day, a deployment took four hours. On a bad day — which happened twice a month — it took two days and the on-call engineer usually lost a night's sleep.

"We were spending 30% of our engineering capacity just maintaining the deployment system," says Sasha Reeve, CTO. "That's a third of a three-person team dedicated to plumbing instead of product. In a competitive market where your model quality can become table stakes overnight, that's existential."

"We were spending 30% of our engineering capacity just maintaining the deployment system. That's a third of a three-person team dedicated to plumbing instead of product."
— Sasha Reeve, CTO, Velocity AI

Evaluating the options

The team evaluated three platforms over a two-week sprint: a managed Kubernetes solution from a hyperscaler, a popular PaaS aimed at larger enterprises, and Launchpad. Their evaluation criteria were strict: time to first deployment, framework compatibility with their FastAPI and Next.js stack, preview environments for feature branches, and pricing that didn't assume Series B revenue.

Launchpad won on every dimension. "We had a real deployment in production during the free trial," Reeve recalls. "Not a toy app — our actual inference API, running on real hardware, with a preview URL we could share with design partners. The other options were still asking us to fill out forms."

The rollout

Velocity AI migrated their three core services to Launchpad over a single weekend in August 2023. The migration required no changes to their application code — Launchpad's framework detection picked up their project structure and pre-configured the build pipeline automatically.

Within the first week, they had:

  • Automated PR preview environments for every open pull request
  • Deploy-on-merge wired to their main branch with automatic rollback on health check failure
  • Launchpad analytics installed and tracking activation events from their onboarding flow
  • Slack notifications for every deployment, pass or fail

The results: six minutes and a fundraise

The headline number — two days to six minutes — understates the real impact. It's not just that deployments are faster. It's that the team's relationship with shipping changed entirely.

"We used to batch features into fortnightly releases because the deployment overhead wasn't worth doing it more often," says Reeve. "Now we ship five to eight times a day. That changes how you build. You get user feedback in hours instead of weeks."

The analytics piece proved unexpectedly valuable during their Series A process. Velocity AI could show investors a real-time dashboard of activation rates, feature adoption, and retention cohorts — without hiring a data engineer or standing up a data warehouse.

"One investor asked us on a Thursday afternoon how our Day-7 retention had trended over the last 90 days, broken by acquisition channel. I pulled up Launchpad on my laptop and showed them the cohort chart in about 20 seconds. That kind of answer would have taken us a week to prepare before."

"Launchpad is the reason we hit our Q3 goals two weeks early — and why our Series A investors were impressed by our engineering velocity."
— Sasha Reeve, CTO, Velocity AI

What's next

Velocity AI closed a $4.2M Series A in November 2023. They've grown to 11 people, moved to Launchpad's Growth plan, and are now using workflow automation to trigger customer success touchpoints based on product usage signals. Their deploy time has crept up to 8 minutes as the codebase has grown — and they consider that a good problem to have.

"We're a product company now," says Reeve. "Not an infrastructure company. Launchpad made that possible."
