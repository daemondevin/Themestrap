Mission

Reduce the distance between idea and impact

Every hour an engineering team spends on infrastructure is an hour they're not spending on the thing that matters: building software people love. Launchpad exists to give those hours back.

Origin

It started with a lost weekend

In October 2021, co-founders Elena Mora, Priya Suresh, and James Obi spent a three-day weekend trying to deploy their side project to production. By Sunday night they had consumed 14 hours of GitHub Actions logs, three Kubernetes manifests, and one very cold pizza.

The side project never shipped. But the idea for Launchpad did.

"We weren't bad engineers," says CEO Elena Mora. "The tools were just designed for teams much bigger than us, with problems much more specific than ours. We wanted something that assumed you were smart, ambitious, and incredibly short on time."

2021

Founded in San Francisco

Elena, Priya, and James quit their jobs and incorporated Launchpad. First commit pushed on a Tuesday at 2am.

2022

Private beta and seed round

Launched private beta with 200 design partners. Raised a $2.4M seed round from Horizon Ventures and a group of angel operators.

2023

Public launch and Series A

Opened to the public in January. Crossed 5,000 customers in 90 days. Raised a $15.6M Series A led by Northstar Capital in Q3.

2024

Analytics platform and enterprise tier

Launched built-in product analytics, A/B testing, and workflow automation. Introduced the Enterprise plan and landed our first Fortune 500 customer.

2025

Launchpad AI and global expansion

Shipped Launchpad AI for anomaly detection and workflow suggestions. Opened EU data residency. Growing to 47 team members across 4 countries.

Values

What we believe

These aren't wall art. They're the filter we apply to every product decision, hiring call, and customer interaction.

01

Builders first

We make decisions by asking: "Does this make a builder's day better?" If the answer isn't obviously yes, we reconsider.

02

Honest by default

Transparent pricing, no hidden limits, no dark patterns. If our product isn't right for you, we'll tell you — and point you somewhere better.

03

Speed is a feature

Every second of latency in our platform costs our customers time and money. We obsess over performance because we know what it costs when it's slow.

04

Ownership, not excuses

When something breaks — and things break — we own it, fix it fast, and write a public post-mortem. No fingerpointing, no spin.

05

Long-term thinking

We're building a company to last decades, not an acqui-hire. Every decision is evaluated on what it means for our customers and team in ten years, not next quarter.

06

Diverse and distributed

Our team spans four countries and fifteen time zones. Async-first isn't a policy for us — it's how we build the product we'd want to use.
